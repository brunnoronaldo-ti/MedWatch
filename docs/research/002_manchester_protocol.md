# research 002: How is a hospital organized?

## How is the patient organization structured?

Patient organizations, such as hospitals or advocacy groups, are structured to deliver care or support, typically featuring a top-level governing board or trustees, senior executive management (e.g., CEO, Medical Director), and various specialized departments. They often adopt functional, divisional, or matrix structures to coordinate patient care.

## Manchester Protocol:

The Manchester Protocol is a globally recognized triage system, created in the United Kingdom in 1994 and adopted by dozens of countries to organize the flow of care in emergency rooms. This method is used in countries such as: Brazil, United Kingdom, Portugal, Netherlands, Sweden, Germany, and Spain. It is worth noting that other countries use similar systems of their own, such as Australia (Australia Triage Scale), Canada (CTAS), and the United States (ESI).

- 🔴 **RED** (Emergency): Immediate care (risk of death).
- 🟠 **ORANGE** (Very Urgent): Service within 10 minutes (high risk).
- 🟡 **YELLOW** (Urgent): Service within 60 minutes (moderate risk).
- 🟢 **GREEN** (Low Urgency): Service within 120 minutes (low risk).
- 🔵 **BLUE** (Non-Urgent): Service within 240 minutes (simple cases).

## another patient organization

**International Patient Safety Goals**: Established by the WHO, these include correct patient identification (use of wristbands with name and date of birth), effective communication between teams, and safety in medication prescription.

**Patient Flow**: Strategies such as *Fast Track* are used to separate low-complexity patients (green/blue) from serious cases, preventing simple cases from overwhelming critical care areas.

### Additional Risk Scales:

- **Braden Scale**: Used to assess the risk of pressure injuries (bedsores) in bedridden patients.
- **Morse Scale**: Assesses the patient's risk of falling during hospitalization.
- **Glasgow Coma Scale**: Used to measure the level of consciousness in cases of trauma or neurological problems.

### Humanized Reception: 

The organization should not be merely technical; it involves active listening and transparent communication with the patient and their family regarding waiting times and next steps.
